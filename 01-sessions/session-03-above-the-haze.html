Session 03 - Above the Haze

Session Note: Clockwork Mysteries, A Fragile Alliance, and Lily Taken

Current Location Hub

  • Drakkenheim: Cosmological Clocktower, Shepherd's Gate
  • Key Sites: Shepherd's Gate

Narrative Acts

Act I: The Clockwork Room

  • The Automatons: The party takes stock of the Cosmological Clocktower's inner clockwork chamber: a pristine, haze-free room of massive gears, counterweights, and bell chains. Several small spheroid automatons wander the floor in silence, maintaining the machinery with metronomic precision, utterly indifferent to the party's presence.

  • A Tower Out of Time: Clank engages the automatons directly, learning they measure time in units called qualons. Cross-referencing their creation dates, the party determines the Cosmological Clocktower is approximately 3,400 years old, predating the founding of the nation entirely. Its inner construction is flawless and alien in quality; the Kessalian stonework on the exterior is a facade added centuries later. The tower absorbed every shatter spell and harpy assault from the previous session without a scratch. The Elven runes carved into the central obelisk describe complex calculations involving planetary conjunctions, something far beyond timekeeping.

  • The Letter: The party finds a letter addressed to the Clockmaster from Archmage Adriana Modiera of the Amethyst Academy, dated shortly before the meteor struck. She writes that a discovery on the Isle of Sky has linked the tower's original machinery to ancient Old Gods sites, and that replacement components were due to arrive from the Inscrutable Tower on September 13th, two days before the meteor fell on September 15th. They never arrived.

Act II: The Rookery and its Treasures

  • The Crimson Countess's Nest: The party ascends into the rookery,a macabre space of bones, filth, skeletal prisoners in cages, and scavenged finery. They loot freely.

  • The Steward's Seal: Found amongst the Crimson Countess's remains alongside a handwritten note: "My child, take this treasure of mine and run. Hide it in a safe place outside the castle. Then flee the city. No matter what happens, know that I will always love you." The Steward's Seal is one of the Seals of Drakkenheim, uniquely able to be attuned by anyone regardless of class. Sable attunes to it during the long rest. She feels the item's frequency connect: close, but not perfectly aligned, as though she is not quite a steward in the way the item understands the word.

  • The Javelin of Lightning: Also recovered from the nest. A magic weapon requiring no attunement, taken by Lily.

  • The Clockmaster's Apartment: The topmost floor sits firmly above the haze. Signs of past adventurers having camped here. The party finds several potions and takes a long rest safely.

Act III: Clocktower, Secured

  • Animal Messenger: Krenzo recruits a one-eyed rat named Bill via Animal Messenger and dispatches him to Emberwood Village with a message for Eldrick: "The clock tower is secure. Followers were here. Come quick. Signed, the Fate Benders." Bill departs by leaping off the top of the tower.

  • Malikas Arrives: Waking in the early hours, Sable investigates a sound below and discovers Malikas and three Amethyst Academy mages accompanied by Malikas already on site, quietly mending the damaged staircase.

  • The Party's Read on Eldrick: The party discusses what Eldrick's interest in the tower likely means. Clank notes that Eldrick seems more focused on Sable than anyone else. The tower's value as a safe rest point above the haze with apparent connections to ancient cosmological machinery makes it an obvious priority. The party reasons that removing the Garmyr serves everyone regardless of alliance.

Act IV: Shepherd's Gate and the Lanterns

  • The Return: Leaving Drakkenheim at dawn, Krenzo spots a creature watching the party from a rooftop: yellow eyes, long white curled fingers, crouched low. It notices him noticing it, fixes its gaze deliberately on Lily, then vanishes.

  • Ansom at the Gate: Ansom Lang meets the party as they emerge through the haze, visibly relieved. Sable reads him as genuine, but also quietly extracting information, and guilty enough about it to be obvious.

  • Petra's Briefing: Petra Lang hangs back to prepare the party before the audience with Elias Drexel. She mentions that Ash may currently be traveling with Moragon, both operating somewhere in the northern sector. She suggests Lily might be the one person who could convince him to come in from the field.

Act V: Audience with Lord Commander

  • The Garrison: The party arrives at the Shepherd's Gate, a fortified city block of stone walls, ballista towers, barracks, and iron portcullises. The Hooded Lanterns drove out the monsters that occupied it the previous year and have been building it into their primary stronghold ever since.

  • Meeting Elias: Elias Drexel is a man in his early sixties. Tall, grey-templed, built like someone who has been dangerous his entire life and shows no signs of stopping. He greets the party warmly and immediately begins probing them for information. Sable reads him as a man who is not particularly gifted at hiding this.

  • The Queen: Clank confirms Queen Lenore is alive but mentally contaminated deep within the haze.

  • Flamekeeper Hannah: Flamekeeper Hannah of the Silver Order joins the conversation to propose a military alliance: the Silver Order will siege Temple Gate from the outside while the Hooded Lanterns strike the Garmyr from within, catching them in a pincer. Lord Commander Elias Drexel is unmoved.

  • The Gates: The party learns the full breakdown of Drakkenheim's five gates. Champions Gate is held by the Followers of the Fallen Flame. College Gate is completely destroyed, nearest to the crater. King's Gate is held by trolls. Shepherd's Gate is held by the Hooded Lanterns. Temple Gate is held by the Garmyr.

  • The Negotiation: The party becomes the deciding voice in the room. They work through the strategic case: the Garmyr are an existential threat to every faction in the city, Ash is actively fighting them in the field, and a pincer assault would be devastating if it lands. Lily pushes hard, invoking her son. The party also floats the Seals of Drakkenheim as a longer parallel path, used to perform a wish powerful enough to clear the Haze entirely.

  • The Deal: Lord Commander Elias Drexel agrees to muster forces within one month. His terms: Silver Order clerics prioritize Hooded Lanterns soldiers for contamination treatment after the assault. Flamekeeper Hannah accepts. The alliance is struck.

Act VI: The Ambush

  • The Garmyr Attack: On the return through Drakkenheim, the party is ambushed. The figure seen from the rooftop had been marking Lily as quarry all along. The party defeats the attacking Garmyr force.

  • Lily Taken: During the fight, a Garmyr druid teleports Lily away. She awakens face to face with The Lord of the Feast. He has a specific and deliberate interest in her.

  • The Regroup: The rest of the party regroups at Shepherd's Gate, battered, and begins mustering help to track her down.
